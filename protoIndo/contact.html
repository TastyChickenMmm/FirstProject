FAQ

Is PIE the oldest language?

Although it is easy to think so, PIE is not the oldest language by far, stretching at most to 6,000 years ago. In contrast, the oldest confidently identified language, Proto-Afroasiatic (the ancestor of many languages from northern Africa and the Middle East), is believed to have been spoken between 12,000 and 18,000 years ago. Even further, estimates for when language first originated range from 50,000 years ago to as much as 1.8 millions years ago.

Is there a proto-language for every language on Earth?

This theory has been proposed before, called Proto-World. However, at this point, such claims have insufficient evidence and have been dismissed as speculation.
