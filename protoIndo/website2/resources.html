PIE Vocabulary

In this section I show a few of my favorite PIE words, and how they changed evolved in other Indo-European languages in each of the major families of descendents. A more complete list can be found on Wikipedia. You will find many weird-looking vowels. The precise sound of each vowel can be found here.

The oldest well-documented language of each family is given, although modern forms are also given for comparison purposes. Languages are followed by the symbol c, standing for circa, which is followed by the approximate time period in which the language was spoken.

Linguistics Glossary

A collection of terms related to/helpful for understanding linguistcs.

Cognate
- a word with the same etymological origin as another, typically through a shared parent language.
Language Family
- A group of languages related through descent from a common ancestral language or parental language.
Parent Language
- The language from which another language derives from.
Proto-language
- The common parent language of a language family.
